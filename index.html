Where to throw your nades?

A data driven analysis of strategic grenade usage in Counter Strike: Global Offensive

By Kyran Adams

Counter Strike: Global Offensive (CS: GO) is a team-based strategic shooter that has not stopped growing in popularity since its release, seven years ago. Counter Strike has nearly 20 years of competitive history, with tournaments now often offering prize pools of more than $1,000,000.

With the increasing popularity of esports comes a increasing importance in advice regarding gameplay strategy. Esports also provides a perfect opportunity to collect and utilize data for analysis, as the game servers can easily collect data on the games.

CS: GO

First, I should give a small explanation of how the game works.

A round is between two teams of five players. In each round, one team, the Terrorists (T), attempts to plant a bomb and defend it until it detonates while the other team, the Counter Terrorists (CT), attempts to stop the bomb plant or defuse it once it's planted. Either team can also win the round by killing all players on the enemy team.

My goal here is to determine locations that are, on average, the best locations to throw grenades. To answer this question, I performed logistic regression on the outcome of the round, using the nade location as a predictor.

Clusters

Because of the likely discontinuous relationship between (x, y) coordinates of utility and their effect on the round, I clustered the utility and assigned them a one-hot encoded categorical value representing which cluster they are in. I used the clustering algorithm Density-based spatial clustering of applications with noise (DBSCAN) which creates clusters that correspond to high density areas of points (colored) while leaving lower density areas (black) as outliers.
